History of Nursing

One of the most famous early figures in modern nursing is Florence Nightingale, known as "The lady with the lamp" for her tireless night-time rounds during the Crimean War. Her dedication helped to transform nursing into a profession grounded in compassion, hygiene, and evidence-based practice — showing that nursing is devotion to human well-being rather than mere business.
